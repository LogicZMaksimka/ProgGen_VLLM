1. North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) - a military alliance of 30 countries from North America and Europe.
2. United States Department of Defense (DoD) - the executive department of the U.S. government responsible for coordinating and supervising all agencies and functions of the government relating directly to national security and the armed forces.
3. Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL) - a militant group and a former unrecognised proto-state that follows a fundamentalist, Salafi jihadist doctrine of Sunni Islam.
4. Russian Armed Forces - the military forces of the Russian Federation, established after the dissolution of the Soviet Union.
5. People's Liberation Army (PLA) - the armed forces of the People's Republic of China and of its founding and ruling political party, the Communist Party of China.
6. United Nations Peacekeeping - a joint operation of the United Nations and the African Union in Sudan's western region.
7. Royal Navy - the United Kingdom's naval warfare force.
8. United States Special Operations Command (USSOCOM) - a unified combatant command of the United States Armed Forces responsible for special operations.
