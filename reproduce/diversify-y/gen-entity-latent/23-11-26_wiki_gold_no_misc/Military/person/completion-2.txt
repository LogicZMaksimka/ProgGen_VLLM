1. George S. Patton - A famous and highly decorated United States Army general during World War II.
2. Dwight D. Eisenhower - A five-star general in the United States Army and Supreme Commander of the Allied Expeditionary Forces in Europe during World War II.
3. Sun Tzu - A Chinese general, military strategist, and philosopher who is traditionally credited as the author of The Art of War, an influential ancient Chinese book on military strategy.
4. Boudica - A queen of the British Celtic Iceni tribe who led an uprising against the occupying forces of the Roman Empire in AD 60 or 61.
5. Simo Häyhä - A Finnish sniper, known as "White Death," who had the highest recorded number of confirmed kills in any major war.
6. Nadezhda Durova - A Russian military officer who, disguised as a man, fought in the Russian army during the Napoleonic wars.
7. Sir John Monash - An Australian army officer who led the Australian Corps during World War I and is considered one of the best Allied generals of the war.
8. T.E. Lawrence - A British archaeologist, army officer, and diplomat who became famous for his role in the Arab Revolt against the Ottoman Empire during World War I.
