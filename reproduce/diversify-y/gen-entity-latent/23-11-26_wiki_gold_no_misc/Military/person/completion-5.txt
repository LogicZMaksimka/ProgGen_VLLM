1. General George S. Patton - American general in the United States Army who commanded the Seventh United States Army in the Mediterranean and European theaters of World War II.

2. Admiral Isoroku Yamamoto - Japanese Marshal Admiral of the Imperial Japanese Navy during World War II.

3. Field Marshal Erwin Rommel - German general and military theorist known as the "Desert Fox" for his leadership in the North African campaign during World War II.

4. Lieutenant General Lewis "Chesty" Puller - United States Marine Corps officer who served in the Pacific theater during World War II and the Korean War, and is one of the most decorated Marines in history.

5. Colonel Guion Bluford - United States Air Force officer and astronaut who was the first African American to travel to space.

6. Major General Smedley Butler - United States Marine Corps officer who was one of the most decorated Marines in history and became an outspoken critic of American military interventionism.

7. Sergeant Alvin York - United States Army sergeant who became famous during World War I for his actions in the Meuse-Argonne Offensive, where he captured 132 German soldiers single-handedly.

8. Brigadier General Sir Nils Olav - Norwegian king penguin who is the mascot and honorary colonel-in-chief of the Norwegian King's Guard.
