1. Grand Canyon - a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in Arizona, known for its geological significance and beauty
2. Andes Mountains - the longest and one of the highest mountain ranges in the world, located across several South American countries
3. Yellowstone National Park - a national park located primarily in the U.S. state of Wyoming, known for its geothermal features and volcanic activity
4. Great Barrier Reef - the world's largest coral reef system, located in the Coral Sea off the coast of Queensland, Australia
5. Mount Everest - the highest mountain in the world, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China (Tibet Autonomous Region)
6. Death Valley - a desert valley located in Eastern California, known for being the hottest and driest place in North America
7. Rio Tinto (river) - a river in southwestern Spain, known for its red color resulting from the presence of iron in the water
8. Mount St. Helens - an active stratovolcano located in the state of Washington, known for its catastrophic eruption in 1980.
