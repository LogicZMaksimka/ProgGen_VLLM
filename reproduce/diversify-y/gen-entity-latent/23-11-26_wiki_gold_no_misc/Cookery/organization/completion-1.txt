1. Le Cordon Bleu - a famous network of culinary and hospitality schools with locations around the world.
2. James Beard Foundation - a nonprofit organization that celebrates, nurtures, and honors chefs and other leaders making America's food culture more delicious, diverse, and sustainable.
3. National Restaurant Association - a restaurant industry business association in the United States, representing more than 380,000 restaurant locations.
4. Slow Food - an organization that promotes local and sustainably sourced food, as well as traditional cooking and food production methods.
5. America's Test Kitchen - a real 2,500 square foot test kitchen located just outside of Boston that is home to more than three dozen full-time cooks and product testers.
6. International Association of Culinary Professionals - an organization that provides education, networking, and professional development opportunities for culinary professionals.
7. Chef's Collaborative - a national advocate for sustainable food that provides a unique forum for American chefs, food professionals, and experts to share knowledge, support each other, and inspire a better food system.
8. Women Chefs & Restaurateurs - an organization that supports women in the culinary industry through education, networking, and mentorship opportunities.
