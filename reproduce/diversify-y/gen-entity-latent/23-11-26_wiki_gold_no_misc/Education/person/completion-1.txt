1. John Dewey - Philosopher, psychologist, and educational reformer who is considered one of the founders of the field of progressive education.
2. Maria Montessori - Italian physician and educator known for developing the Montessori method of education, which emphasizes child-centered learning and individualized instruction.
3. Paulo Freire - Brazilian educator and philosopher known for his influential work in critical pedagogy, particularly his book "Pedagogy of the Oppressed."
4. Horace Mann - American educator and politician who played a key role in promoting public education and is often referred to as the "Father of the Common School Movement."
5. Michelle Rhee - American educator and advocate for education reform who served as the chancellor of the District of Columbia Public Schools from 2007 to 2010.
6. Howard Gardner - American psychologist known for his theory of multiple intelligences, which has had a significant impact on educational practices and curriculum development.
7. W.E.B. Du Bois - American sociologist, historian, and civil rights activist who was a strong advocate for African American education and co-founded the NAACP.
8. Diane Ravitch - American educational historian and former U.S. Assistant Secretary of Education known for her advocacy for public education and criticism of education reform policies.
