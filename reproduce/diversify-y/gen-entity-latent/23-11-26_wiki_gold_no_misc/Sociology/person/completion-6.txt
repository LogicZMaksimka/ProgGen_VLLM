1. Max Weber - German sociologist, philosopher, and political economist known for his work on the Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism.
2. Harriet Martineau - English social theorist and writer, often considered to be one of the first female sociologists.
3. W.E.B. Du Bois - American sociologist, historian, and civil rights activist who co-founded the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP).
4. Jane Addams - American social worker, sociologist, and reformer, known for her pioneering work in the field of social work and her efforts to improve the lives of the urban poor.
5. Talcott Parsons - American sociologist known for his work in the areas of social theory and structural functionalism.
6. C. Wright Mills - American sociologist known for his critical analysis of power and authority in American society.
7. Dorothy Swaine Thomas - American sociologist and demographer known for her work on family and gender issues in sociology.
8. Erving Goffman - Canadian-American sociologist known for his studies of face-to-face interaction and the presentation of self in everyday life.
