1. Transportation Security Administration (TSA) - The TSA is an organization within the U.S. Department of Homeland Security that is responsible for overseeing security in the transportation systems of the United States.
2. International Air Transport Association (IATA) - The IATA is a trade association of the world's airlines that represents over 80% of air traffic.
3. American Automobile Association (AAA) - The AAA is a federation of motor clubs throughout North America that provide roadside assistance, insurance, and other services to their members.
4. National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) - The NHTSA is an agency of the U.S. government that is responsible for setting and enforcing safety standards for motor vehicles and roadways.
5. Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) - The FAA is a regulatory agency of the U.S. Department of Transportation that oversees civil aviation and ensures the safety and efficiency of the national airspace system.
6. Amtrak - Amtrak is the national passenger railroad service in the United States, providing intercity and long-distance travel.
7. European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) - The EASA is an agency of the European Union that is responsible for civilian aviation safety and regulatory oversight.
8. National Railroad Passenger Corporation (Amtrak) - Amtrak is a passenger railroad service that provides intercity and long-distance travel in the United States.
