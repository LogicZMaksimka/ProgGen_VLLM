1. European Space Agency (ESA) - an intergovernmental organization dedicated to the exploration of space, with 22 member states
2. National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) - the United States government agency responsible for the nation's civilian space program and for aeronautics and aerospace research
3. Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) - a federally funded research and development center and NASA field center in California
4. Space Telescope Science Institute (STScI) - operates the science program for the Hubble Space Telescope and its successor, the James Webb Space Telescope
5. Astronomical Society of the Pacific (ASP) - a scientific and educational organization, founded in 1889, dedicated to increasing understanding and appreciation of astronomy
6. European Southern Observatory (ESO) - an intergovernmental astronomy organization supported by 16 countries in Europe and South America
7. Harvard-Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics (CfA) - a research institute at Harvard University and the Smithsonian Institution
8. Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) - a research institute in Heidelberg, Germany, dedicated to the study of astronomy and astrophysics.
