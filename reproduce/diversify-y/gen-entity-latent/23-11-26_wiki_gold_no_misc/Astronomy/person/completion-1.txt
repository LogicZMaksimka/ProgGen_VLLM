1. Galileo Galilei - Italian astronomer, physicist and engineer, sometimes referred to as the "father of observational astronomy".
2. Carl Sagan - American astronomer, cosmologist, author, and science communicator known for popularizing astronomy and other scientific fields.
3. Vera Rubin - American astronomer who pioneered work on galaxy rotation rates, providing evidence for the existence of dark matter.
4. Edwin Hubble - American astronomer known for his contributions to the understanding of the universe, including the discovery of the expansion of the universe.
5. Maria Mitchell - American astronomer who was the first professional female astronomer in the United States and the first woman to discover a comet.
6. Annie Jump Cannon - American astronomer who developed the system for classifying stars based on their spectral characteristics.
7. Clyde Tombaugh - American astronomer who discovered the dwarf planet Pluto and several asteroids.
8. Johannes Kepler - German astronomer known for his laws of planetary motion, which provided a foundation for the later development of the theory of universal gravitation by Isaac Newton.
