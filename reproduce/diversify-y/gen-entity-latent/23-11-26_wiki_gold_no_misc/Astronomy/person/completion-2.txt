1. Galileo Galilei - Italian astronomer, physicist and engineer, sometimes described as a polymath, from Pisa, Italy, who played a major role in the scientific revolution during the Renaissance.

2. Edwin Hubble - American astronomer who played a crucial role in establishing the field of extragalactic astronomy and is generally regarded as one of the most important observational cosmologists of the 20th century.

3. Vera Rubin - American astronomer who pioneered work on galaxy rotation rates, providing evidence of the existence of dark matter.

4. Carl Sagan - American astronomer, planetary scientist, cosmologist, astrophysicist, astrobiologist, author, science popularizer, and science communicator in astronomy and other natural sciences.

5. Annie Jump Cannon - American astronomer whose cataloging work was instrumental in the development of contemporary stellar classification.

6. Nicolaus Copernicus - Renaissance-era polymath who formulated a model of the universe that placed the Sun rather than Earth at the center of the universe.

7. Cecilia Payne-Gaposchkin - British-American astronomer and astrophysicist who, in 1925, was the first to demonstrate that the Sun was mainly composed of hydrogen, contradicting accepted wisdom at the time.

8. Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar - Indian American astrophysicist who was awarded the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1983 for his theoretical studies of the physical processes of importance to the structure and evolution of the stars.
