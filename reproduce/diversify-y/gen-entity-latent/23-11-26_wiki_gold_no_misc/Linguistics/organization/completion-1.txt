1. International Phonetic Association (IPA) - an organization that promotes the scientific study of phonetics and the various practical applications of that science.
2. Linguistic Society of America (LSA) - the largest national professional society representing the field of linguistics.
3. European Association of Linguistic Typology (EALT) - an international organization that promotes the study of linguistic diversity and typological research.
4. American Association for Applied Linguistics (AAAL) - a professional organization for individuals interested in applied linguistics.
5. Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL) - an international scientific and professional society for people working on problems involving natural language and computation.
6. Linguistic Data Consortium (LDC) - an open consortium of universities, companies and government research laboratories that creates, collects and distributes speech and text databases.
7. Center for Applied Linguistics (CAL) - a private non-profit organization that promotes access, equality and mutual understandings among all people.
8. Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics - an institute dedicated to studying the psychological and neurobiological mechanisms of language processing.
