1. Mount Olympus - In Greek mythology, this mountain was considered the home of the twelve Olympian gods.
2. Hades - In Greek mythology, Hades refers both to the ancient Greek underworld and the god of the underworld.
3. Mount Parnassus - In Greek mythology, this mountain was sacred to the god Apollo and the Muses.
4. Avalon - In Arthurian legend, Avalon is a legendary island featured in various medieval texts as the place where King Arthur's sword Excalibur was forged.
5. Valhalla - In Norse mythology, Valhalla is a majestic, enormous hall located in Asgard, ruled over by the god Odin.
6. Atlantis - In Greek mythology, Atlantis is a legendary island first mentioned by the ancient Greek philosopher Plato.
7. Elysium - In Greek mythology, Elysium is a conception of the afterlife that developed over time and was maintained by some Greek religious and philosophical sects and cults.
8. Jotunheim - In Norse mythology, Jotunheim is one of the Nine Worlds and the homeland of the Jotnar, the frost giants.
