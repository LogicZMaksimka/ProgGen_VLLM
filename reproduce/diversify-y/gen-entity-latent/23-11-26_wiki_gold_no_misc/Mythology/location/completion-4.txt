1. Mount Olympus - In Greek mythology, Mount Olympus is often referred to as the home of the gods and the highest mountain in Greece.
2. Asgard - In Norse mythology, Asgard is one of the Nine Realms and is the home of the Aesir gods.
3. Avalon - In Arthurian legend, Avalon is a mythical island often associated with the afterlife and the realm of King Arthur.
4. Hades - In Greek mythology, Hades is both the name of the god of the underworld and the underworld itself.
5. Land of the Dead - In various mythologies, the Land of the Dead is a location where the spirits of the deceased reside.
6. Valhalla - In Norse mythology, Valhalla is a majestic hall located in Asgard where warriors who died in battle are taken to by valkyries.
7. Tartarus - In Greek mythology, Tartarus is a deep abyss that is used as a dungeon of torment and suffering for the wicked.
8. Elysium - In Greek mythology, Elysium is a paradise where heroes and the virtuous reside after death.
