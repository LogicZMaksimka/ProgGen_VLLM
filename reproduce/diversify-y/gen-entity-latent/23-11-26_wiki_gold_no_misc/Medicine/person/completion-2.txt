1. Florence Nightingale - An influential figure in the field of nursing, known for her contributions to patient care and hospital reform.

2. Dr. Anthony Fauci - A prominent immunologist and director of the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases, known for his expertise in infectious diseases and leadership during public health crises.

3. Dr. Elizabeth Blackwell - The first woman to receive a medical degree in the United States, and a pioneer for women in the field of medicine.

4. Dr. Paul Farmer - A physician and anthropologist known for his work in global health and founding the organization Partners In Health.

5. Dr. Helen Brooke Taussig - A pediatric cardiologist known for her contributions to the development of the first successful open-heart surgery for children with congenital heart defects.

6. Dr. William Osler - A prominent physician and one of the founding professors of Johns Hopkins Hospital, known for his contributions to medical education and research.

7. Dr. Mae Jemison - A physician and astronaut, known for becoming the first African American woman to travel in space.

8. Dr. Virginia Apgar - A pioneering obstetrical anesthetist known for developing the Apgar Score, a standardized method for evaluating the health of newborns.
