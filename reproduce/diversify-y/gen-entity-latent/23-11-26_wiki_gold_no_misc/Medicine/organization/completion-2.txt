1. World Health Organization (WHO) - A specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health.
2. American Medical Association (AMA) - A professional association for physicians and medical students in the United States.
3. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) - A national public health institute in the United States.
4. Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF) - An international humanitarian medical organization that provides medical assistance to people affected by conflict, epidemics, and natural disasters.
5. National Institutes of Health (NIH) - The primary agency of the United States government responsible for biomedical and public health research.
6. Doctors Without Borders - A medical humanitarian organization that provides medical assistance to people affected by armed conflict, epidemics, and natural disasters.
7. Red Cross - A humanitarian organization that provides emergency assistance, disaster relief, and education in the United States and around the world.
8. Pan American Health Organization (PAHO) - An international public health agency working to improve health and living standards of the countries of the Americas.
