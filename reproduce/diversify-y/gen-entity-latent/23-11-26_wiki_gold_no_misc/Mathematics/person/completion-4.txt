1. Euclid - Ancient Greek mathematician often referred to as the "father of geometry".
2. Hypatia - Ancient Greek mathematician and philosopher known for her contributions to algebra and number theory.
3. Ada Lovelace - English mathematician and writer, often credited as the first computer programmer for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.
4. Carl Friedrich Gauss - German mathematician known for his work in number theory, algebra, and statistics.
5. Emmy Noether - German mathematician known for her groundbreaking contributions to abstract algebra and theoretical physics.
6. Alan Turing - English mathematician and computer scientist known for his work on the Turing machine and his contribution to the field of computer science.
7. Mary Cartwright - British mathematician known for her work in chaos theory, differential equations, and the study of non-linear dynamic systems.
8. Bernhard Riemann - German mathematician known for his contributions to analysis, differential geometry, number theory, and the study of Riemann surfaces.
