1. Christopher Columbus - Italian explorer who completed four voyages across the Atlantic Ocean, opening the way for widespread European exploration and colonization of the Americas.
2. Amelia Earhart - American aviation pioneer and author who was the first female aviator to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.
3. Marco Polo - Venetian merchant and explorer who traveled from Europe to Asia in the 13th century and served as an emissary for the Mongol Empire.
4. Ibn Battuta - Moroccan scholar and traveler who is widely recognized for his extensive travels, which covered nearly the entirety of the Islamic world and beyond.
5. Sacagawea - Lemhi Shoshone woman who accompanied the Lewis and Clark Expedition as a guide and interpreter, contributing to the success of their exploration of the Western United States.
6. Yuri Gagarin - Soviet cosmonaut and the first human to journey into outer space, completing one orbit of the Earth in 1961.
7. Freya Stark - British-Italian explorer and travel writer who extensively explored and documented the Middle East, particularly in regions such as Iran, Arabia, and Afghanistan.
8. Nellie Bly - American journalist and adventurer known for her record-breaking trip around the world in 72 days, as well as her investigative reporting and groundbreaking work in the field of travel journalism.
