1. Christopher Columbus - Italian explorer and navigator who completed four voyages across the Atlantic Ocean, opening the way for widespread European exploration and the eventual conquest of the Americas.
2. Amelia Earhart - American aviation pioneer and author who was the first female aviator to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.
3. Marco Polo - Venetian merchant, explorer, and writer who traveled through Asia along the Silk Road and introduced Europeans to Central Asia and China.
4. Sacagawea - Lemhi Shoshone woman who helped the Lewis and Clark Expedition achieve its chartered mission objectives by exploring the Louisiana Territory.
5. Ibn Battuta - Moroccan scholar and explorer who widely traveled the medieval world and documented his experiences in a book titled "Rihla."
6. Vasco da Gama - Portuguese explorer and the first European to reach India by sea, linking Europe and Asia for the first time by an ocean route.
7. Nellie Bly - American journalist who was widely known for her record-breaking trip around the world in 72 days, mimicking the fictional journey of Phileas Fogg in Jules Verne's "Around the World in Eighty Days."
8. Yuri Gagarin - Soviet pilot and cosmonaut who became the first human to journey into outer space when his Vostok spacecraft completed an orbit of the Earth on April 12, 1961.
