1. United States Capitol - The home of the United States Congress and the seat of the legislative branch of the U.S. federal government, located in Washington, D.C.
2. 10 Downing Street - The residence and office of the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, located in London, England.
3. Kremlin - The fortified complex at the heart of Moscow, Russia, which serves as the official residence of the President of the Russian Federation.
4. White House - The official residence and workplace of the President of the United States, located in Washington, D.C.
5. Tiananmen Square - A large public square in Beijing, China, that has been the site of many important political events and protests.
6. Parliament House, Canberra - The meeting place of the Parliament of Australia, located in the capital city of Canberra, Australia.
7. Bundestag - The national parliament of Germany, located in Berlin, the capital city of Germany.
8. European Parliament - The directly elected parliamentary institution of the European Union, located in Strasbourg, France, and Brussels, Belgium.
