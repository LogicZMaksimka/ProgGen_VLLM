1. Washington D.C. - The capital city of the United States and the location of the federal government.
2. Brussels - The capital city of Belgium and the de facto capital of the European Union.
3. Tiananmen Square - A public square in Beijing, China, known for its political significance.
4. Downing Street - A street in London, England, known for housing the official residences of the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom and the Chancellor of the Exchequer.
5. United Nations Headquarters - The headquarters of the United Nations, located in New York City, USA.
6. Kremlin - A fortified complex in Moscow, Russia, that serves as the official residence of the President of Russia.
7. Capitol Hill - A historic neighborhood in Washington D.C. that is home to the United States Congress.
8. European Parliament - The legislative branch of the European Union, located in Strasbourg, France, and Brussels, Belgium.
