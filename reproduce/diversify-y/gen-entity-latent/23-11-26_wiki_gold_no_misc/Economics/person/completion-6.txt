1. Adam Smith - Scottish economist and philosopher, known as the father of modern economics
2. John Maynard Keynes - British economist whose ideas greatly influenced modern economic theory and policy
3. Milton Friedman - American economist and Nobel Prize winner known for his work on monetarist economics and advocacy of free-market capitalism
4. Paul Krugman - American economist and Nobel Prize winner known for his work on international trade and economic geography
5. Amartya Sen - Indian economist and Nobel Prize winner known for his work on welfare economics and the capability approach
6. Janet Yellen - American economist and former Chair of the Federal Reserve, and the first female Secretary of the Treasury
7. Joseph Stiglitz - American economist and Nobel Prize winner known for his work on asymmetric information and development economics
8. Dambisa Moyo - Zambian economist and author known for her work on international finance and macroeconomics
