1. Florence Nightingale - a celebrated English nurse known for her work during the Crimean War and for laying the foundation of professional nursing.
2. Jonas Salk - an American medical researcher and virologist who developed the first successful polio vaccine.
3. Elizabeth Blackwell - the first woman to receive a medical degree in the United States and a pioneer for women in the field of medicine.
4. Hippocrates - an ancient Greek physician often referred to as the "Father of Medicine" and known for his contributions to the field of medicine.
5. Clara Barton - a pioneering nurse who founded the American Red Cross and played a crucial role in providing medical aid during the American Civil War.
6. Dr. Paul Farmer - an American physician and anthropologist known for his humanitarian work in providing healthcare to impoverished communities around the world.
7. Dr. Sanjay Gupta - a prominent American neurosurgeon and medical reporter for CNN, known for his contributions to public health education and advocacy.
8. Sir William Osler - a Canadian physician and one of the four founding professors of Johns Hopkins Hospital, known for his significant contributions to medical education and healthcare.
