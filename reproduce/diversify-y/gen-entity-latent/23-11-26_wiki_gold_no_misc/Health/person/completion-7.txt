1. Florence Nightingale - known as the founder of modern nursing, she made significant contributions to public health and healthcare reform.

2. Dr. Anthony Fauci - an American physician and immunologist who has served as the director of the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases since 1984.

3. Dr. Jane Goodall - a British primatologist, ethologist, and anthropologist known for her pioneering research on chimpanzees and her environmental conservation efforts.

4. Deepak Chopra - an Indian-American author, speaker, and alternative medicine advocate who has written extensively on mind-body healing and holistic health.

5. Dr. Sanjay Gupta - a neurosurgeon and medical correspondent for CNN, known for his reporting on public health issues and medical breakthroughs.

6. Mary Leakey - a British paleoanthropologist who made important discoveries about early human evolution and the origins of human health and disease.

7. Dr. Mehmet Oz - a Turkish-American cardiothoracic surgeon, author, and television personality known for his popular show "The Dr. Oz Show" and his advocacy for preventive medicine.

8. Dr. Paul Farmer - an American physician and anthropologist who co-founded the international healthcare organization Partners In Health and has worked extensively on providing healthcare to impoverished communities around the world.
