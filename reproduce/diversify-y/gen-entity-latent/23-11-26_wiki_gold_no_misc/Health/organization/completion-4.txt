1. World Health Organization (WHO) - A specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health.
2. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) - A national public health institute in the United States.
3. Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF) - An international humanitarian medical organization that provides emergency medical assistance to people affected by conflict, epidemics, and natural disasters.
4. National Institutes of Health (NIH) - A part of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, it is the primary agency for conducting and supporting medical research.
5. American Red Cross - A humanitarian organization that provides emergency assistance, disaster relief, and education in the United States.
6. Doctors Without Borders - A non-governmental organization that delivers emergency medical aid to people affected by armed conflict, epidemics, natural and man-made disasters, and exclusion from healthcare.
7. The Lancet - A weekly peer-reviewed general medical journal and a top global publisher in medical and health research.
8. Health Canada - A department of the Government of Canada responsible for national public health.
