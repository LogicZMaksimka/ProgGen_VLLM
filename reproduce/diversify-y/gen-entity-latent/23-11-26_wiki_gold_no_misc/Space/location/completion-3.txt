1. Kennedy Space Center - A spaceport located in Florida, USA, used for launches by NASA and private companies.
2. Baikonur Cosmodrome - The world's first and largest spaceport, located in Kazakhstan, used for Russian and international space missions.
3. International Space Station (ISS) - A habitable artificial satellite in low Earth orbit, serving as a space environment research laboratory.
4. Mars - The fourth planet from the Sun in our solar system, often targeted for future human exploration and colonization.
5. Hubble Space Telescope - A space telescope located in low Earth orbit, providing valuable images and data for astronomy research.
6. Cape Canaveral Space Force Station - A launch site located on the east coast of Florida, USA, used for military and civilian space missions.
7. Challenger Deep - The deepest known point in the Earth's seabed, located in the Pacific Ocean's Mariana Trench, often compared to space exploration due to its extreme conditions.
8. Kuiper Belt - A region of the outer Solar System, extending beyond the orbit of Neptune, that contains numerous small icy bodies and dwarf planets.
