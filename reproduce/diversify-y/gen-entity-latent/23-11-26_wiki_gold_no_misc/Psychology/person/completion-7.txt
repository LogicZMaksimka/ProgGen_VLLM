1. Sigmund Freud - A renowned Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis.
2. B.F. Skinner - An American psychologist known for his work in behaviorism and operant conditioning.
3. Carl Rogers - An influential American psychologist and one of the founders of humanistic psychology.
4. Ivan Pavlov - A Russian physiologist known for his research on classical conditioning, which laid the foundation for behaviorism.
5. Mary Ainsworth - A developmental psychologist known for her work on attachment theory and the "strange situation" experiment.
6. Jean Piaget - A Swiss psychologist known for his work in child development and cognitive theory.
7. Karen Horney - A German psychoanalyst known for her theories on neurosis and feminist psychology.
8. Aaron Beck - An American psychiatrist known for developing cognitive therapy, which has become a widely used approach in treating depression and anxiety disorders.
