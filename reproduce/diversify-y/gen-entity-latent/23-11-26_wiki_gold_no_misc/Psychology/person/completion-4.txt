1. Sigmund Freud - Austrian neurologist and the founder of psychoanalysis, known for his theories on the unconscious mind and the role of childhood experiences in shaping personality.
2. B.F. Skinner - American psychologist known for his work in behaviorism and operant conditioning, and for his development of the Skinner box, a tool for studying animal behavior.
3. Carl Jung - Swiss psychiatrist and psychologist, known for his work on analytical psychology and his concepts of the collective unconscious and archetypes.
4. Jean Piaget - Swiss psychologist known for his work in child development and cognitive theory, particularly his theory of the four stages of cognitive development.
5. Mary Ainsworth - Canadian-American developmental psychologist known for her work on attachment theory and the "Strange Situation" experimental procedure to assess attachment styles in infants.
6. Aaron Beck - American psychiatrist and psychotherapist known for his development of cognitive therapy, particularly his work on cognitive distortions and his cognitive triad theory of depression.
7. Elizabeth Loftus - American cognitive psychologist known for her research on human memory, particularly for her work on the misinformation effect and false memories.
8. Karen Horney - German psychoanalyst known for her theories on neurosis and personality, particularly her criticisms of Freudian psychoanalysis and her emphasis on the role of social and cultural factors in shaping personality.
