2. National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) - NAMI is an American organization dedicated to improving the lives of individuals and families affected by mental illness, through support, education, and advocacy.

3. World Health Organization (WHO) - The WHO is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health, including mental health and psychological well-being.

4. American Counseling Association (ACA) - The ACA is the world's largest association exclusively representing professional counselors in various practice settings, promoting the professional development of counselors and the counseling profession.

5. National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) - NIMH is one of 27 institutes and centers that make up the National Institutes of Health, conducting research on mental illnesses and disorders with the aim of improving mental health treatment and prevention.

6. British Psychological Society (BPS) - The BPS is the representative body for psychology and psychologists in the United Kingdom, working to promote excellence and ethical practice in the science, education, and application of psychology.
