1. American Psychological Association (APA) - a professional organization representing psychologists in the United States.

2. National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) - a government organization that conducts research on mental health issues.

3. World Health Organization (WHO) - a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health.

4. Harvard University Department of Psychology - a department within Harvard University dedicated to the study of psychology with a focus on research and education.

5. British Psychological Society (BPS) - the professional body for psychologists in the UK, responsible for the development, promotion, and application of psychology.

6. National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) - an American organization dedicated to providing support and resources for individuals and families affected by mental illness.

7. International Society for Traumatic Stress Studies (ISTSS) - an organization focused on promoting the advancement and exchange of knowledge about traumatic stress.

8. American Board of Professional Psychology (ABPP) - an organization that certifies and credentials psychologists who demonstrate competency in various areas of psychology practice.
