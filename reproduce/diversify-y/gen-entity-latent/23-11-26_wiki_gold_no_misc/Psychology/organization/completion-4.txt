1. American Psychological Association (APA) - the largest scientific and professional organization of psychologists in the United States, with over 118,000 members.
2. British Psychological Society (BPS) - the professional body for psychologists in the UK, representing and supporting over 60,000 members.
3. National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH) - a leading federal agency for research on mental disorders, part of the National Institutes of Health in the United States.
4. American Psychiatric Association (APA) - a professional organization of psychiatrists in the United States, with over 38,000 members.
5. World Health Organization (WHO) - a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health, including mental health and psychological well-being.
6. Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology (SIOP) - a professional organization for industrial-organizational psychologists, promoting the science and practice of the field.
7. National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI) - a grassroots mental health organization in the United States, providing support, education, and advocacy for individuals and families affected by mental illness.
8. Mental Health Foundation (MHF) - a charitable organization in the UK that works to improve mental health and well-being, supporting research, campaigns, and community programs.
