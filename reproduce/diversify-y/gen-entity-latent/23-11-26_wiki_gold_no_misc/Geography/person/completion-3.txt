1. Christopher Columbus - Italian explorer who completed four voyages across the Atlantic Ocean, opening the way for widespread European exploration and the eventual conquest of the Americas.
2. Ptolemy - Greco-Roman mathematician, astronomer, geographer, and astrologer. He is best known for his work on geography and cartography.
3. Alexander von Humboldt - German naturalist and explorer, known for his travels to the Americas and his significant contributions to the field of physical geography.
4. Marie Tharp - American geologist and oceanographic cartographer who created the first scientific map of the ocean floor, revolutionizing the field of marine geology.
5. David Thompson - British-Canadian fur trader, surveyor, and map-maker, known for his extensive explorations and mapping of Western North America.
6. Sonia Sotomayor - American lawyer and Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States, known for her role in landmark cases related to environmental and geographical issues.
7. Edward Bransfield - British Royal Navy officer and explorer who is credited with the discovery of the Antarctic mainland.
8. Ellen Churchill Semple - American geographer known for her pioneering work in the field of human geography, particularly in the study of environmental determinism.
