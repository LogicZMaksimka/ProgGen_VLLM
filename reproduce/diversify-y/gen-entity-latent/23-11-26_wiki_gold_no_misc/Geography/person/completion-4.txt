1. Marco Polo - Italian merchant, explorer, and writer who traveled extensively in Asia and documented his experiences in his famous book "The Travels of Marco Polo".
2. Jacques Cousteau - French naval officer, explorer, and filmmaker known for his pioneering work in marine conservation and the development of the Aqua-Lung, a type of scuba gear.
3. Amelia Earhart - American aviation pioneer and author who was the first female aviator to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.
4. David Livingstone - Scottish physician, explorer, and missionary who extensively explored southern and central Africa, and was the first European to see the Victoria Falls.
5. Sir Edmund Hillary - New Zealand mountaineer, explorer, and philanthropist who, along with Tenzing Norgay, became the first climbers to reach the summit of Mount Everest.
6. Captain James Cook - British explorer, navigator, and cartographer who made three voyages to the Pacific Ocean and is credited with the first recorded European contact with the eastern coastline of Australia and the Hawaiian Islands.
7. Lewis and Clark - Meriwether Lewis and William Clark were American explorers who led the famous Lewis and Clark Expedition to explore the newly acquired Louisiana Purchase and the Pacific Northwest.
8. Fridtjof Nansen - Norwegian explorer, scientist, and diplomat who led the first expedition to cross the Greenland interior and later became a prominent advocate for refugees and humanitarian causes.
