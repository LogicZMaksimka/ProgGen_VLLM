1. Edmund Hillary - the first person confirmed to have reached the summit of Mount Everest
2. Ferdinand Magellan - the Portuguese explorer who organized the Spanish expedition that resulted in the first circumnavigation of the Earth
3. Marco Polo - a Venetian merchant and explorer who traveled to the Mongol Empire and wrote about his experiences in "The Travels of Marco Polo"
4. Sacagawea - a Lemhi Shoshone woman who accompanied the Lewis and Clark Expedition as an interpreter and guide
5. David Livingstone - a Scottish missionary and explorer who extensively explored Africa and wrote extensively about his travels
6. John Cabot - an Italian explorer who is known for his voyages to North America and is considered the first European to explore parts of the continent since the Norse expeditions
7. Vasco da Gama - a Portuguese explorer who led the first successful maritime voyage from Europe to India, opening an important commercial sea route
8. Nellie Bly - an American journalist who traveled around the world in 72 days, beating the fictional record of the character Phileas Fogg in Jules Verne's "Around the World in Eighty Days"
