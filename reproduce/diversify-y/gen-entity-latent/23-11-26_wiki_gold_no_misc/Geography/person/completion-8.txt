1. Alexander von Humboldt - German geographer and explorer known for his work in mapping and exploring South America.
2. Jacques-Yves Cousteau - French oceanographer and explorer who co-developed the Aqua-Lung and was a pioneer in marine conservation.
3. Marie Tharp - American geologist and oceanographic cartographer who created the first scientific map of the Atlantic Ocean floor.
4. Alfred Russel Wallace - British naturalist and explorer who independently developed the theory of evolution through natural selection.
5. Gertrude Bell - English writer, archaeologist, and political officer who played a key role in the establishment of modern Iraq.
6. David Livingstone - Scottish missionary and explorer who extensively traveled and documented the African continent in the 19th century.
7. Tenzing Norgay - Nepalese-Indian Sherpa mountaineer who, along with Sir Edmund Hillary, was one of the first two individuals to reach the summit of Mount Everest.
8. John Wesley Powell - American geologist and explorer known for his exploration of the Colorado River and the Grand Canyon.
