1. Mount Everest - the highest mountain in the world, located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and China.
2. Great Barrier Reef - the world's largest coral reef system, located in the Coral Sea off the coast of Queensland, Australia.
3. Sahara Desert - the largest hot desert in the world, covering much of North Africa.
4. Lake Baikal - the world's deepest and oldest freshwater lake, located in Siberia, Russia.
5. Amazon Rainforest - the largest tropical rainforest in the world, situated in South America, primarily in Brazil.
6. The Nile River - the longest river in the world, flowing through northeastern Africa.
7. The Grand Canyon - a steep-sided canyon carved by the Colorado River in Arizona, United States.
8. The Great Wall of China - a series of fortifications made of stone, brick, tamped earth, wood, and other materials, constructed along the northern borders of China.
